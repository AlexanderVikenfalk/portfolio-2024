History

On a more personal note, I grew up in a small fishing village in the south of Sweden. Not much happens in a village like that, but the water and the calmness are nice. When most other kids did more daring activities, like sailing or playing ice hockey, I would go to the only place in the village which had internet access: the local library.

I spent a lot of time there, exploring this new, exciting world and installing games and applications on the library computer - to the chagrin of the librarian. At home my father, also a programmer, installed Linux on our only computer, teaching me early on to have patience, to solve problems and to be curious about technology. As time went by I tried to find my own way in life, but I always kept on coming back to programming and computers. After high school I studied to become a network engineer and started my career in IT support shortly after. Then my exploration instinct got the better of me and told me to try my wings in development. I enrolled for programs for both Frontend and Backend studies in a vocational university in Sweden and moved to Berlin to complete my courses remotely. This was in 2016, and I've been here ever since.
